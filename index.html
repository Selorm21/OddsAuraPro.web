Bet Responsibly

Betting is an exciting way to add a thrill to your favorite games, but it’s important to do so responsibly. Follow these tips to maintain a safe and enjoyable betting experience:

Guidelines for Responsible Betting

  • Only bet what you can afford to lose: Treat any money spent on betting as part of your entertainment budget.
  • Set limits on your time and spending: Decide in advance how much time and money you are willing to dedicate to betting.
  • Never chase losses: Accept that losing is part of the experience and avoid the temptation to recover losses by betting more.
  • Bet for entertainment, not as a source of income: Always treat betting as a hobby, not as a way to make money.

Self-Assessment Questions

  • Do you ever spend more on betting than you intended?
  • Does betting interfere with your daily responsibilities or relationships?
  • Have you borrowed money or sold possessions to fund your bets?
  • Do you feel anxious, stressed, or guilty about your betting habits?

Resources for Support

  • Gamblers Anonymous: Find support groups and resources.
  • National Gambling Helpline: Call 1-800-GAMBLER (1-800-426-2537) for confidential assistance.
  • Check for local resources in your region for additional support.

Legal Age Warning

Betting is only allowed for individuals above the legal gambling age in your jurisdiction. Ensure you comply with these laws before participating in any betting activities.
